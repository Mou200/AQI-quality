Predicting Air Quality with

Empowering informed decisions through advanced prediction of surface PM levels. We leverage satellite data, ground measurements, and machine learning to provide accurate and reliable air quality forecasts. Explore our methodology and contribute to a healthier future. Our Mission & Methodology

AODPredict harnesses satellite imagery, ground measurements, and reanalysis data to accurately predict surface PM levels. Using advanced machine learning models, we aim to provide reliable air quality insights that support environmental health initiatives and policy decisions.
INSAT Satellite Data
INSAT provides crucial AOD spatial maps. Its geostationary orbit offers continuous monitoring, essential for tracking pollution dynamics over the Indian subcontinent.
CPCB Ground Measurements
CPCB's ground-based network delivers accurate surface PM data. This data is vital for calibrating and validating AODPredict's PM level predictions.
MERRA-2 Reanalysis Data
MERRA-2 offers comprehensive atmospheric variables. Integrating this data enhances the model's ability to predict surface PM levels under varying meteorological conditions.
Integrated Data Approach
AODPredict leverages the strengths of INSAT, CPCB, and MERRA-2. This integrated approach ensures robust and reliable surface PM level predictions.
Random Forest Algorithm
We employ the Random Forest algorithm, known for its robustness and accuracy in handling complex environmental datasets. It integrates satellite, ground, and reanalysis data.
Data Fusion Process
Our model training involves fusing data from multiple sources to create a comprehensive dataset. This ensures the model learns from diverse environmental conditions.
Feature Importance Analysis
Feature importance analysis helps us understand which variables, such as AOD or wind speed, are most critical in predicting surface PM levels accurately.
Cross-Validation Technique
To ensure reliability, we use cross-validation to rigorously test the model's performance. This helps prevent overfitting and ensures accurate PM level predictions.

Understanding Our Surface PM Level Prediction Process

Our prediction process integrates satellite data, ground measurements, and atmospheric reanalysis to accurately estimate surface PM levels. This involves data acquisition, preprocessing, model training, validation, and continuous refinement to ensure reliable air quality predictions. We strive to provide timely and accurate information for environmental monitoring and decision-making.
Data Acquisition and Preprocessing
We gather data from INSAT satellites, CPCB ground stations, and MERRA-2 reanalysis. Preprocessing involves cleaning, calibrating, and integrating these diverse datasets to ensure data quality and consistency for accurate model training.
Learn More
Model Training and Application
A machine learning model, like Random Forest, is trained using satellite AOD, ground-based PM data, and atmospheric variables. The trained model predicts surface PM levels, providing real-time air quality estimates for various locations.
Learn More
Validation and Evaluation
The model's predictions are validated against CPCB ground truth measurements to assess accuracy. Evaluation metrics, such as RMSE and R-squared, are used to quantify the model's performance and identify areas for improvement.

Model Accuracy: PM2.5 Predictions
Our model demonstrates high accuracy in predicting PM2.5 levels, validated against CPCB data. This ensures reliable air quality information.
Capturing Seasonal PM Trends
The model accurately captures seasonal PM variations, crucial for long-term air quality management and understanding pollution patterns.
Spatial Error Distribution Analysis
Spatial analysis reveals areas of high and low prediction accuracy, guiding future model refinements for enhanced regional performance.
Performance Benchmarking Against Baseline
AODPredict outperforms baseline models, showcasing its advanced capabilities in accurately predicting surface PM levels across diverse conditions.
